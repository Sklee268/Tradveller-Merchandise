§ 01 / Identity

Identity & Story Tradveller is the uniform of the new generation — built for people who pursue freedom with purpose. This is the brand’s reason to exist, in our own words.

Designed for movement. Inspired by mindset.

At Tradveller, we believe life is a journey between two worlds — the one you build and the one you explore.

Born from the spirit of trade and travel, our brand represents those who pursue freedom with purpose — the thinkers, the doers, the creators who trade not just assets, but ideas, time, and energy to design a life without borders.

Our clothing is made for modern mobility: minimalist, functional, and timeless. Each piece blends performance and simplicity — adaptable enough for a long flight, a spontaneous trip, or a quiet morning of focus.

We value less, but better. Every material, every seam, every detail serves a reason — to keep you comfortable, confident, and ready to move.

Tradveller is not about luxury; it’s about freedom — the freedom to work anywhere, live everywhere, and stay grounded while chasing the world ahead.

/ 01 · Voice

Quiet, considered, never loud.

Speak like a designer, not a marketer. Sentence case, short lines, no exclamation marks. The brand persuades by being right, not by shouting.

/ 02 · Aesthetic

Black, white, one purple.

The shop is essentially monochrome. Brand purple appears only as the bull mark and a single accent state — never as decoration, never as a fill of large areas.

/ 03 · Product

Less, but better.

Four categories. Five-piece collections. Tight curation. Every piece earns its place — if it doesn’t move with you, it doesn’t ship.

/ 04 · Audience

Thinkers in transit.

People who trade ideas, time, and energy. Remote-first, design-literate, allergic to logomania. We make pieces that belong on a long flight and a quiet morning.

§ 02 / Logo

Logo system One bull, one wordmark, and the lockups that pair them. The full official set ships in four forms × three colourways — everything you need for any surface.

Primary · Horizontal

Bull + wordmark, side-by-side

Default for the site header, footer, hangtags, business communications, and any surface wider than 80 mm.

Vertical

Stacked, hero use

For centred placements that need air around them — full chest, full back, posters, hangtag covers, vertical banners.

Icon · Solo

The bull, alone

For caps, embroidery, app icons, sticker drops, favicons, and any tight footprint. The hero of the brand.

Naming pattern

Tradveller [Category]

Always lead with the parent brand. Examples: Tradveller T-Shirt, Tradveller Jackets, Tradveller Graphic Oversize T. For special drops, append the drop name after the category. Never invent product nicknames.

Pricing format

RM 120

Currency in caps, hard space before the number, no decimals on round prices. RM 30, RM 120. Strike-through old price on sale: RM 80  RM 60.

✓ DoKeep · Reinforce
  • Use sentence case in body copy. “Our clothing is made for modern mobility.” Never title-case marketing copy.
  • Lead with the parent name. “Tradveller T-Shirt,” not “T-Shirt”. Every product carries the brand.
  • Keep the bull purple, only. Brand purple appears as the bull mark or a single accent state — never as a background fill or large area of colour.
  • RM with a hard space. RM 60, always. Never RM60 or $60.
  • Photograph against neutral grounds. Stone, sand, charcoal, paper. The clothing carries the contrast; the set doesn’t need to.
✕ Don’tReject · Rework
  • Don’t use emoji or crypto slang. No moons, rockets, “gm,” or “WAGMI.” The aesthetic dies on contact.
  • Don’t stretch or recolour the wordmark. Horizontal at 100% width, brand purple or white only.
  • Don’t use exclamation marks in headlines. Confidence whispers — only Only 3 pieces left! stock callouts are exempt.
  • Don’t add gradients or background patterns to the shop. Photography is the only decoration. The grid does the rest.
  • Don’t price with decimals on round numbers. “RM 60”, never “RM 60.00”. Decimals only when there are real cents.
